For the first photomontage which I call, “Greed’s Apathy,” represents how capitalism is one of the main problems in industrialized society. Specifically in the U.S., where the difference in economic status is so distinct and intense, money not only gives one power but also a newfound greed and distorted perspective on others. I try to accomplish this through the figure in this work, the Capitalist man, who walks around the city square with a disregard for the homeless people surrounding him. Blinded with a Starbucks bag over his head, he moves forward thinking about what next to do with his wealth. The money flying away from him is all that he spends so far, but note that none of those banknotes will ever reach the lower class lying on the floor. In Photoshop, I modified the hue and saturation of the figures in adjustment layers and clipping masks to fit the dark setting. I also overlaid the city image over a nebula background with a lower opacity to give the city a distinct blue smoke. Then I created a field blur for the things behind the Capitalist man for shallow depth of field. All these photos were taken from Unsplash Creative Commons images.

This second work, “Indifference to Destruction,” portrays the average congressperson or lobbyist who claims the climate crisis is a hoax to further push their agendas. The destruction this entails for the environment is widely apparent, but if one of these people were to view it with their own eyes they would have no concern to change their actions. This photomontage tries to capture this heartless point-of-view through the combination of images of environmental disasters and the back of the decision-maker’s head. In Photoshop, I overlaid images of the melting iceberg, burning forest, and rising skyscrapers in adjustment layers. The skyscrapers are above the rest since industry and business holds more importance than the environment. I also blended the figures to have a similar color shade as the smoke in the sky with the black and white adjustment layer and increasing the yellow and cyan values. Finally I used a hard light on the decision-maker to represent the indifference. These photos were taken from Unsplash and Pixabay Creative Commons images.
